Eleanor Vance

Born in the rugged landscapes of the Pacific Northwest, Eleanor Vance has dedicated two decades to the pursuit of architectural stillness. Her work explores the intersection of brutalist forms and the organic decay of time.

Her photographs are held in private collections across Europe and North America, and she has been a regular contributor to editorial titles such as Cereal, Kinfolk, and The New York Times.
